Why early screening matters

Most project problems are visible early — if you know where to look

Many projects do not fail because the battery is impossible. They fail because the customer value, contract revenue and financing case were never tested together — early enough, and in one place.

A public-information Pre-DD screen turns that uncertainty into a structured decision: advance, optimise, redesign, or stop — before EPC quotation, legal structuring or financing conversations begin.

The Pre-DD workflow

The F1-F4 Pre-DD Framework

Every project is screened through four connected questions, from public information only. Together they test whether a hybrid energy idea is ready to advance — or what has to be resolved first.

F1

Approval Delta

Does the project fit within the existing approval pathway, or does it trigger new issues around disturbance, MMP / licence variation, water, fire, heritage, biodiversity, closure or ownership chain?

F2

Site Suitability

Where could PV, BESS, PCS and interconnection equipment realistically sit, and what constraints are visible from public maps, mine layout, watercourses, roads, pipelines and closure areas?

F3

Microgrid Boundary

How would PV, BESS, diesel generation, PCS, EMS and mine loads connect, and what SLD, genset, switchboard, control, metering and load data is required before reliability can be assessed?

F4

Financeability Readiness

Which commercial structure is worth validating, and what must be proven before the project can move toward EPC pricing, term sheet, lender model, CP mapping or financial close preparation?

Inside F4

Inside F4: the financeability lens

Financeability readiness (F4) itself breaks down into five connected questions. Most financeability problems show up where two of them meet.

01

Technical fit

Is the PV, BESS, PCS, GFM and diesel-backup sizing matched to the real load — not the peak?

02

Customer avoided cost

What does the customer actually stop paying, compared with diesel or grid supply?

03

Contract revenue

How much of that value becomes stable, contracted SPV revenue — not just a saving?

04

Carbon value

Is the carbon benefit real and visible — and kept out of double-counted revenue?

05

Bankability

Does the fixed revenue actually support debt service, DSCR and what a lender will ask?

Screening principles

My point of view

01

Customer avoided cost is not the same as project company revenue. The customer’s whole saving is not what the SPV gets paid — only the contracted fee is.

02

A project can create customer savings and still fail the lender case. A bank underwrites coverage and contracted cash flow, not goodwill.

03

Carbon value should be visible, but not double-counted. I keep it on the customer side and out of base SPV revenue.

04

Contracted EaaS revenue matters more than speculative arbitrage. Fixed payments carry debt; market upside is a bonus, not a base case.

05

Early feasibility should help people decide whether to proceed, optimise, redesign or stop. The point is a clear decision, not a perfect forecast.

About

Heliovulcan provides independent early-stage project screening for industrial hybrid energy — PV+BESS, diesel-PV-BESS microgrids and weak-grid mine and industrial sites.

Heliovulcan helps owners and project sponsors test whether an early PV+BESS or hybrid-energy opportunity is ready to advance. Its F1-F4 Pre-DD workflow covers approval delta, site suitability, microgrid boundary and financeability readiness.

The work combines public-map interpretation, candidate energy-zone hypotheses, approval-pathway questions, microgrid boundary questions and financeability logic. Clients receive an early, structured view of what is known, what remains unproven and which specialist work or owner data should come next.

Heliovulcan provides early-stage screening and decision support, not detailed EPC design, formal due diligence or regulated professional advice. The goal is to make the first project decision sharper: is the idea worth advancing, what needs to change, and what evidence is required next?
