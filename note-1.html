Project note 01 EaaS / bankability Working notebook

Customer savings are not bankable revenue

One of the most important lessons in early BESS feasibility is this:

Customer savings are not the same as project company revenue.

A solar + BESS project may reduce diesel fuel use, lower generator O&M, improve reliability and create carbon benefits for the customer. That is the customer avoided-cost pool.

But a project company does not receive the whole avoided-cost pool. It only receives what the contract allows it to receive.

That distinction matters.

A project can look attractive from the customer side and still fail the lender case. The customer may save money, but if the SPV’s contracted service fee is too low, too uncertain or too dependent on upside revenue, the project may not support debt.

This is why I separate the analysis into three cases:

S1 · base case

Fixed EaaS service fee only

The core base case. This is the revenue a lender can actually look at.

S2 · upside

EaaS fee plus BTM upside

Useful upside, but not something I would treat as the bank base case.

S3 · comparison

Pure BTM arbitrage

A comparison case. Usually not suitable as the foundation for weak-grid or mining project finance.

The lesson is simple:

Customer value is necessary, but contracted revenue is what carries debt.
