Industry Maps & Frameworks

Visual maps for mining solar, BESS and hybrid-energy projects

Five compact frameworks showing how industrial energy projects move from an opportunity to a buildable, financeable and operable system. Use them as discussion maps, not substitutes for project-specific engineering or due diligence.

Explore the maps ↓Read Project Notes

Map 01

Mining Hybrid Energy Development Process

A mine solar, battery and firming concept only becomes a project after commercial need, site rights, approvals, operating architecture and finance are aligned.

Energy problemCost, reliability, expansion
→
Pre-DD screenFatal gaps and evidence
→
Site + approvalsBuildable pathway
→
System designSolar, BESS, engines, grid
→
Commercial structureOwnership and payment
→
Build + operateIntegrated performance

The development sequence is iterative, but the dependency is real: a technical design cannot repair missing land rights, and attractive customer savings cannot finance an SPV without a payment contract.

Map 03

Who Does What in a BESS Project

Most delivery risk sits between parties. Clear ownership of each decision, interface and remedy matters as much as the component specification.

Owner / sponsorBusiness case, capital, governance
Network / operatorConnection, studies, compliance
OEMBattery, PCS, warranty, controls
EPC / integratorDesign, BoP, interfaces, commissioning
Offtaker / customerPayment, load, operating access
Lenders + advisersRisk allocation and independent review

Every shared function should have one design owner, one integration owner, one test and one contractual remedy.

Map 04

Remote Mine Power Architecture

The goal is not to add technologies. It is to coordinate energy, fast response and firm capacity around the mine’s production load.

Solar PVLow-cost daytime energy
BESSShifting, ramps, peaks, reserve
Diesel / gasDeep firming and contingency
Microgrid controllerDispatch, protection and stability
Mine loadProduction and critical services
Grid / future optionImport, export or later connection

Reliability is a binding constraint. The controller must make the portfolio behave as one power station.
