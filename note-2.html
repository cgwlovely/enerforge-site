Project note 02 Project finance Working notebook

A BESS project can create value and still need financing optimisation

Not every “failed” project is actually a bad project.

Sometimes a project creates real value for the customer, but the financing structure does not yet work.

For example, a weak-grid industrial solar + BESS project may reduce diesel cost and give the customer meaningful annual savings. From the customer side, the project makes sense.

But once that value is translated into fixed EaaS revenue and tested against debt service, the lender case may still fail. DSCR may be below target. Debt capacity may be lower than the assumed debt size. The tail-year repayment may be too tight.

That does not necessarily mean the technical solution should be abandoned.

It may mean the project needs one of four things:

  • Lower debt gearing
  • A stronger fixed payment or minimum payment
  • Lower EPC cost
  • Better contract support or customer credit support

This is why I prefer the phrase:

Financing structure requires optimisation.

It is more accurate than saying the project is simply “not viable.”

The project may still be worth developing further — but not under the current commercial and financing assumptions.
