Project note 05 EaaS / bankability Working notebook

Why S1 matters more than S2 upside

In a BESS model, upside revenue is attractive.

BTM arbitrage, spare capacity, flexible dispatch, market participation and carbon benefits can all make the project look better.

But for early project finance, I care most about S1.

S1 is the fixed EaaS service fee. It is the customer payment that is closest to contracted revenue. It is the part of the model that can support the base case.

S2 may improve returns, but it depends on extra assumptions:

  • Does the project company have dispatch rights?
  • Who keeps the arbitrage value?
  • Is spare capacity actually available?
  • Are price spreads stable?
  • Does the customer accept this operating model?

That is why I treat S2 as upside, not the foundation.

For weak-grid mining and industrial projects, the base case should not depend on speculative arbitrage. It should depend on a clear customer payment for a clear service.

The simple rule:

S1 carries the debt. S2 improves the story.
