Fountain Head Hybrid Energy Project — Preliminary Development & Financeability Review

A public-information, desktop preliminary review of a remote Northern Territory gold-mine hybrid-energy opportunity — testing site suitability, approval risk, microgrid boundary and financeability readiness for a solar PV + battery storage (BESS) + diesel hybrid, before serious development cost is committed.

Executive summary F1–F4 readiness Key findings Interactive maps Key risks Next steps PDF reports Sources

Executive summary

This review screens whether a 60% renewable diesel–PV–BESS hybrid pathway is a more useful base case for the Fountain Head gold-mine site than a short-life standalone option or a 100% renewable upper-bound case. It combines public-map interpretation, a preliminary siting opportunity-and-constraint screen, approval-pathway questions, microgrid boundary questions and financeability observations into a single early read.

On the public information available, scenario S4 is the case selected for hourly and commercial validation. The mapping identifies preliminary PV and BESS/PCS search areas, constraints, review areas and locations requiring owner confirmation. These are not equipment footprints or approved sites. The output is a clearer next-step pathway — not a design, financing decision or investment recommendation.

What this is

This is an independent, public-information desktop screen. It is not formal due diligence, EPC design, a bankable feasibility study, lender due diligence, or legal, financial, tax or investment advice. All figures and zones are indicative and must be validated against owner data before any commercial decision.

Project context

Fountain Head is a remote, weak-grid gold-mine setting in the Northern Territory, historically dependent on diesel generation. Remote and off-grid mines typically want to reduce diesel exposure, lower operating cost and improve emissions performance — but the first question is not technical. It is whether the ownership, contract, approval and owner-saving story holds together at all.

Indicative annual demand is in the order of ~30 GWh/yr, reconstructed from public environmental-impact material. The review tests a solar PV + BESS + diesel hybrid under a third-party / Energy-as-a-Service style structure, across a range of renewable shares.

Key findings

  • S4 is the scenario selected for further validation — not a final design or investment recommendation.
  • The siting output is a desktop search-area screen. It separates preliminary opportunities, strong constraints, review-required areas and owner-confirmation areas; it does not identify approved buildable land.
  • More renewable was not automatically better in the screening model. The 100% annual-renewable target is a capital-heavy upper bound that requires hourly reliability and equipment validation.
  • A modelled 1.30x DSCR constraint is not lender approval. Debt was resized in each scenario to that simplified constraint.
  • Further work is required before any decision — owner data room, hourly dispatch modelling, EPC pricing and specialist due diligence.

Public information sources

This desktop review is built only from public and indicative information, including:

  • NT EPA Fountain Head assessment register — project history, EIS documents and assessment material.
  • Fountain Head draft EIS main report and GHG assessment — indicative annual electricity demand and peak load.
  • Public company reporting (PNX Metals / Sunrise) — disclosed PV and BESS starting points.
  • Global Solar Atlas — Pine Creek area solar-yield assumptions.
  • Analyst assumptions — diesel price, genset O&M, EPC benchmarks, gearing and tenor.
Evidence boundary

Linked sources support the public project context and selected starting inputs. Scenario sizing, preliminary search areas, diesel price, EPC benchmarks, gearing, tenor, DSCR and owner-outcome calculations are Heliovulcan screening assumptions. They have not been confirmed by the project owner, an EPC contractor or a lender.

Key risks and open questions

  • Approval delta (F1): whether the project fits the existing approval pathway, or triggers new issues around disturbance, MMP / licence variation, water, fire, heritage, biodiversity, closure or ownership chain.
  • Site suitability (F2): usable area, setbacks, watercourses, roads, pipelines and closure areas constraining where PV, BESS, PCS and interconnection can realistically sit.
  • Microgrid boundary (F3): how PV, BESS, diesel, PCS, EMS and mine loads connect, and the SLD, switchboard, metering and load data required before reliability can be assessed.
  • Financeability (F4): mine-life and contract tenor risk, residual buy-out, debt sizing and whether the contracted structure can support debt service.

Open questions

  • Which land, access and disturbance areas can the owner make available for energy infrastructure?
  • What hourly load, diesel dispatch and reliability criteria must the hybrid system meet?
  • Which party owns and operates the PV, BESS, diesel plant, connection assets and control system?
  • What contract tenor, payment floor, residual treatment and credit support could support finance?

Recommended next steps

  1. Open an owner data room — confirmed mine plan, load and diesel data.
  2. Run hourly dispatch modelling to validate the renewable share and reliability.
  3. Define scenario-specific PV, BESS, diesel, fuel-use and dispatch schedules, then obtain indicative EPC pricing for the configuration selected for validation.
  4. Commission specialist due diligence (technical, approvals, legal, commercial).
  5. Develop the commercial structure and a CP / financial-close pathway.
