Project note 09 Model building Working notebook

A good model should create better questions

I do not think an early-stage model should pretend to know everything.

At the beginning of a project, many things are uncertain:

  • Load data
  • Diesel price
  • EPC cost
  • Battery degradation
  • Customer credit
  • Contract term
  • Grid-forming requirement
  • Debt terms
  • Insurance
  • Permitting

A good early model should not hide that uncertainty. It should turn uncertainty into better questions.

Questions like:

  • What EPC quote do we need to verify first?
  • What minimum payment would make the project bankable?
  • What happens if debt gearing drops from 70% to 60%?
  • Is carbon value owned by the customer or shared with the SPV?
  • Is the battery sized for average load or peak load?
  • Is the upside revenue actually dispatchable?

That is why I think of EnerForge less as a “calculator” and more as a project-intelligence workflow.

The output is not only a number.

The output is a clearer next conversation.
