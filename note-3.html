Project note 03 BESS feasibility Working notebook

Weak-grid mines are not just an energy problem

A weak-grid mine does not think about energy like a normal grid-connected office building.

Diesel is not just a fuel source. It is backup capacity, reliability insurance, peak support and operational security.

That means a solar + BESS project should not be designed as if the goal is to remove all diesel immediately.

A more practical question is:

Which part of the load can be served economically and reliably by solar and storage, while diesel remains available for peaks and backup?

That is why I do not size BESS only against short-duration peak load. Mines and industrial sites can have peaks several times higher than their average load. If the system is sized only to chase rare peaks, the project can become too expensive very quickly.

A better early-stage approach is to understand:

  • Average load
  • Night load
  • Predictable energy demand
  • Diesel displacement target
  • Backup requirements
  • PCS and grid-forming needs
  • Contractable revenue

The technical question and the financing question are connected.

A BESS can solve a reliability problem, but it still needs a bankable commercial structure.
